Professional Skepticism

A balanced, questioning and alert mindset

Meaning of Professional Skepticism

Defined under SA 200.

Professional skepticism is an attitude that includes a questioning mind, alertness to conditions which may indicate possible misstatement due to fraud or error, and a critical assessment of audit evidence.

Why Professional Skepticism Is Necessary

Core audit attitude.

Professional skepticism is necessary because:
  • Audit evidence is persuasive, not conclusive
  • Management may be biased
  • Fraud may exist despite past experience

Without skepticism, the auditor may overlook unusual circumstances or misinterpret evidence.

Balanced Mindset

Neither suspicious nor trusting.

The auditor should:
  • Not assume that management is dishonest
  • Not assume that management is honest

This balanced approach ensures objectivity and professional judgment.

Application of Professional Skepticism

Throughout the audit.

Professional skepticism is applied while:
  • Planning the audit
  • Performing risk assessment
  • Evaluating audit evidence
  • Forming audit opinion

Exam Perspective

Frequently tested concept.

In exams, ICAI expects students to:
  • Define professional skepticism clearly
  • Mention “questioning mind” and “alertness”
  • Explain balanced approach

Avoid writing: “Auditor should suspect management”.
