Introduction of SA 200

Foundation of audit objectives.

SA 200 establishes the overall objectives of the independent auditor and explains the nature and scope of an audit, role and responsibility of the auditor, reasonable assurance, professional judgment and professional skepticism.

Overall Objectives of the Auditor

Two-fold responsibility.

As per SA 200, the auditor’s objectives are:
  • To obtain reasonable assurance
  • To report on the financial statements

→ Read in depth

Reasonable Assurance

High but not absolute assurance.

Audit provides a high level of assurance that financial statements are free from material misstatement, but not absolute assurance, due to inherent limitations of audit.

→ Read in depth

Inherent Limitations of Audit

Why some misstatements may remain.

Inherent limitations arise due to the nature of financial reporting, audit procedures, and time and cost constraints, resulting in an unavoidable risk that some misstatements may not be detected.

→ Read in depth

Material Misstatement

Misstatements that influence decisions.

A material misstatement is one that could reasonably influence the economic decisions of users and may arise due to fraud or error.

→ Read in depth

Auditor vs Management Responsibility

Clear division of roles.

Management is responsible for preparation of financial statements and internal controls, whereas the auditor’s responsibility is to obtain reasonable assurance and express an opinion.

→ Read in depth

Professional Skepticism

Balanced and questioning mindset.

Professional skepticism is an attitude that includes a questioning mind, alertness to conditions indicating misstatement, and a critical assessment of audit evidence.

→ Read in depth

Professional Judgment

Experience-based decisions.

Professional judgment involves application of relevant training, knowledge and experience in making informed decisions throughout the audit.

Conduct of Audit as per SAs

Mandatory compliance.

The auditor shall comply with all relevant Standards on Auditing, understand their entire text, and not claim compliance unless fully complied with.

→ Read in depth

Ethical Requirements

Core professional values.

Auditor shall comply with ethical requirements including integrity, objectivity, professional competence, confidentiality, professional behaviour and independence.

→ Read in depth

Audit Risk

Risk of inappropriate opinion.

Audit risk is the risk that the auditor expresses an inappropriate opinion and includes inherent risk, control risk and detection risk.

→ Read in depth

Scope of SA 200

Framework standard.

SA 200 applies to all audits of financial statements and provides the framework within which all other Standards on Auditing operate.
