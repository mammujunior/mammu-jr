Professional Judgment

Applying knowledge and experience to make informed audit decisions

Meaning of Professional Judgment

Defined under SA 200.

Professional judgment is the application of relevant training, knowledge and experience, within the context provided by auditing, accounting and ethical standards, in making informed decisions about the courses of action that are appropriate in the circumstances of the audit.

Why Professional Judgment Is Necessary

Audit cannot be mechanical.

An audit cannot be performed by following a checklist alone. Professional judgment is necessary because:
  • Financial reporting involves estimates and assumptions
  • Audit evidence is not always conclusive
  • Different situations require different audit responses

Areas Where Judgment Is Applied

Throughout the audit process.

Professional judgment is applied in areas such as:
  • Determining materiality
  • Assessing audit risks
  • Deciding nature, timing and extent of audit procedures
  • Evaluating audit evidence
  • Forming audit opinion

Judgment Is Not Guesswork

Important distinction.

Professional judgment is:
  • Based on knowledge and experience
  • Applied objectively
  • Supported by evidence and reasoning

It is not arbitrary decision-making or personal opinion without basis.

Link with Professional Skepticism

Closely related concepts.

Professional skepticism ensures alertness and questioning, while professional judgment enables the auditor to make informed decisions based on that alertness.

Exam Perspective

How to score marks.

In exams:
  • Define professional judgment clearly
  • Mention application of training, knowledge and experience
  • Explain that audit is not mechanical

Avoid writing: “Auditor uses personal opinion”.
