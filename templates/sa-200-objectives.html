Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor

The purpose behind every audit engagement

What Are the Overall Objectives?

Defined under SA 200.

As per SA 200, the overall objectives of the independent auditor are to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement and to report on the financial statements in accordance with the auditor’s findings.

Objective 1 – To Obtain Reasonable Assurance

Primary audit objective.

The auditor’s first objective is to obtain reasonable assurance that the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, whether due to:
  • Fraud, or
  • Error

This assurance enables the auditor to express an opinion on the financial statements.

Objective 2 – To Report on Financial Statements

Communication responsibility.

The auditor is required to report on the financial statements and communicate as required by the Standards on Auditing, in accordance with the auditor’s findings.

Reporting enhances the degree of confidence of intended users in the financial statements.

Important Exam Line

Frequently tested.

The auditor’s opinion enhances the degree of confidence of intended users in the financial statements.

Link with Reasonable Assurance

Conceptual connection.

The objective of obtaining reasonable assurance forms the foundation of the entire audit process and explains why audit does not provide absolute assurance.

Exam Perspective

How to write answers.

In exams:
  • Clearly mention both objectives
  • Use the phrase “financial statements as a whole”
  • Always link assurance with reporting

Avoid vague statements like “to check correctness of accounts”.
