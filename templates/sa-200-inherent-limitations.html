Inherent Limitations of an Audit

Why an audit cannot detect all misstatements

What Are Inherent Limitations?

Core concept in SA 200.

Inherent limitations are those limitations that arise from the nature of audit and financial reporting itself.
Because of these limitations, an audit cannot provide absolute assurance that financial statements are free from misstatement.

Nature of Financial Reporting

Judgment and estimates.

Financial statements involve:
  • Use of accounting estimates
  • Management judgment
  • Subjective matters

Hence, some misstatements may not be objectively determinable.

Nature of Audit Procedures

Evidence is persuasive.

Audit evidence is generally persuasive, not conclusive.

Also:
  • Audit sampling is used
  • Auditor cannot examine all information

Time & Cost Constraints

Practical limitation.

An audit must be conducted within reasonable time and cost.

The auditor cannot verify 100% of transactions.

Resulting Risk

Unavoidable detection risk.

Due to inherent limitations, there is an unavoidable risk that some material misstatements may not be detected, even when the audit is properly planned and performed.

Exam Perspective

Frequently tested area.

In exams, students should clearly mention:
  • Nature of financial reporting
  • Nature of audit procedures
  • Time and cost constraints

Always conclude: An audit cannot provide absolute assurance.
