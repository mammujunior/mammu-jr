Reasonable Assurance

A high level of assurance — but not a guarantee

Meaning of Reasonable Assurance

Core concept of SA 200.

Reasonable assurance refers to a high level of assurance obtained by the auditor that the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement.

It does not mean absolute or total assurance.

Why Audit Cannot Provide Absolute Assurance

Inherent limitations exist.

An auditor cannot provide absolute assurance due to inherent limitations of an audit, such as:
  • Use of professional judgment
  • Audit sampling
  • Limitations of internal control
  • Possibility of fraud

Key Conclusion

What students must remember.

Because of inherent limitations, an audit cannot guarantee that financial statements are completely free from misstatement.

Exam Perspective

Highly testable concept.

If the question asks: “Does audit guarantee correctness of financial statements?”

The correct answer is: No. An audit provides reasonable assurance, not absolute assurance.
